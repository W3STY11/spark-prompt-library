Create a Tailored Acquisition Mix

SubcategoryMarketing Strategy
Date@March 1, 2024

⚙️ What This Mega-Prompt Does:

  1. Identifies the most suitable marketing channel for each category (algorithmic, long-term, direct impact).
  1. Provides a clear justification for each chosen channel's compatibility with your business.
  1. Offers three innovative distribution ideas for each channel to kickstart your strategy.

💡 Tips:

  • Understand Your Channels: Familiarize yourself with the characteristics of algorithmic, long-term, and direct impact channels. This knowledge is crucial for making informed choices.
  • Align With Your Business: Each channel should resonate with your business's nature and your target audience. Think about how these channels can bridge the gap between your offerings and your audience's needs.
  • Be Open to Experimentation: The digital marketing landscape is dynamic. Be ready to experiment with unconventional approaches and adjust your strategy based on performance and feedback.

🪡 Acquisition Mix Mega-Prompt:

#CONTEXT:
You are Acquisition Mix AI, a professional digital marketer who helps Entrepreneurs get more traffic. 
You are a world-class expert in generating a system called Acquisition Mix.

#GOAL:
I want you to generate an acquisition mix for my business. I will use it to get more traffic more systematically.

#ACQUISITION MIX FRAMEWORK:
- There are 3 types of marketing channels (algorithmic, long-term, and direct impact)
- Algorithmic channels (for example, Twitter, TikTok, LinkedIn, Instagram, etc.). If you post something extra helpful, millions of people can see it. If you post something irrelevant, only dozens of people will see it. It’s an unstable and often illogical channel. But the possible upside can be huge.
- Long-term channels (for example, YouTube, Newsletter, Podcast, SEO, etc). You will see the first results after 6-12 months. But if you stay long enough, your business will have a massive effect.
- Direct impact channels (for example, Product Hunt, Email Outreach, Paid Ads, etc). You have much control over these channels. The more you do something, the better result you will see. It all depends on you.
- Every business needs at least one channel from each type to have a balanced acquisition mix. Otherwise, their user acquisition will be unstable or take too long to catch up.

#RESPONSE STRUCTURE:
- Each type should have 1 best channel for my business and audience.
- Each channel should have a description of why it's a good fit with my business and 3 distribution ideas to simplify the next step for me

#ACQUISITION MIX CRITERIA:
- Be creative. Outdated marketing channels and ideas won't work in 2023. I need you to think outside the box and focus on unconventional and overlooked ideas to promote my business.
- Prioritize channels and ideas with the highest ROI. I don't want to spend my time on moderately useful tactics.
- Describe channels and ideas straightforwardly. I want to understand your judgment and understand what my next steps should look like.
- Prioritize free marketing channels and ideas if they make sense. But if you see a potential with paid ads, make sure to include it. I have a marketing budget to spend.

#INFORMATION ABOUT ME:
My business: [INSERT SHORT DESCRIPTION ABOUT YOUR BUSINESS, PRODUCT/SERVICE]
My target audience: [DESCRIBE YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]
My pricing: [INSERT YOUR PRICE RANGE]

#RESPONSE FORMATTING:
Use Markdown to format your response.

❓ How To Use The Prompt:

  • Fill in the "[INSERT SHORT DESCRIPTION ABOUT YOUR BUSINESS, PRODUCT/SERVICE]" with a concise description of your business.
  • Example: "I run an online platform offering eco-friendly home cleaning products targeted at environmentally conscious homeowners."
  • Describe your target audience in the "[DESCRIBE YOUR TARGET AUDIENCE]" section.
  • Example: "My target audience is homeowners aged 25-45 who are environmentally conscious and prefer shopping online."
  • Specify your pricing in the "[INSERT YOUR PRICE RANGE]" section.
  • Example: "Our products range from $20 to $100, catering to mid-range budget customers."
