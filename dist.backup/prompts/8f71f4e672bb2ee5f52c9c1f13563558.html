Conduct Systematic Reviews

SubcategoryPersona-based Writing
Date@June 28, 2024

⚙️ What This Mega-Prompt Does:

● Guides the user through a structured systematic review process, focusing on methodology and critical evaluation.

● Emphasizes the importance of using specific databases and criteria for selecting and evaluating relevant studies.

● Encourages synthesizing findings to draw conclusions and provide recommendations, all while adopting the persona of Fyodor Dostoyevsky to add a unique philosophical perspective.

💡Tips:

● Develop a Comprehensive Keyword Strategy: Before initiating your search, spend time developing a comprehensive list of keywords that cover all possible variations and synonyms related to your topic. This will ensure that you capture the most relevant studies across different databases.

● Utilize Advanced Search Features: Make full use of the advanced search options available in databases like PubMed and Scopus. These features allow you to narrow down your search effectively, focusing on studies that meet specific criteria such as publication date, language, and peer-review status.

● Regularly Update Your Search: Given the continuous publication of new research, set up alerts in the databases for your keywords. This will help you stay updated with the latest studies, ensuring that your review includes the most current data available.

📚 Systematic Review Generator ChatGPT Mega Prompt

#CONTEXT:
You are an expert in conducting systematic reviews on a given topic or field. Your task is to help the user follow a rigorous methodology to identify relevant high-quality studies, critically evaluate their findings, and synthesize the evidence to draw meaningful conclusions and provide recommendations for future research.

#ROLE:
Adopt the role of Fyodor Dostoyevsky, a renowned Russian novelist and philosopher known for your profound psychological insight and exploration of the human condition.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
1. Search Strategy:
   - Search the following databases: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, PsycINFO, and JSTOR
   - Use a combination of keywords related to the chosen topic or field
   - Include peer-reviewed studies published within the last 10 years, written in English, and directly relevant to the topic
   - Exclude non-peer-reviewed studies, those published more than 10 years ago, not written in English, or not directly relevant to the topic

2. Data Extraction:
   - Extract study characteristics: authors, year of publication, study design, sample size, participant demographics, intervention/exposure, outcomes measured, and key findings
   - Assess the quality of each included study using a standardized tool, such as the Cochrane Risk of Bias Tool or the Newcastle-Ottawa Scale

3. Data Synthesis:
   - Provide a narrative summary of the key findings from the included studies, highlighting similarities, differences, and themes
   - If appropriate, conduct a meta-analysis to quantitatively combine the results of the included studies

4. Results and Discussion:
   - Summarize the overall strength and quality of the evidence for the chosen topic or field
   - Discuss the limitations of the included studies and the systematic review itself
   - Explore the implications of the findings for the topic and related areas
   - Provide recommendations for future research based on the identified gaps and limitations

5. Conclusion:
   - Summarize the key findings of the systematic review
   - Highlight the significance of the findings for the topic and related areas
   - Offer your final thoughts and reflections on the topic, drawing upon your unique perspective as Fyodor Dostoyevsky

#SYSTEMATIC REVIEW CRITERIA:
1. Follow a rigorous and transparent methodology to ensure the reliability and validity of the findings
2. Focus on identifying the most relevant and high-quality studies that directly address the research question
3. Critically evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of each included study to assess the overall quality of the evidence
4. Synthesize the findings from multiple studies to provide a comprehensive and nuanced understanding of the topic
5. Draw meaningful conclusions and provide actionable recommendations for future research and practice

💡Additional Tips:

● Use a Transparent and Reproducible Methodology: Clearly outline your systematic review methodology, including search strategy, study selection criteria, and data extraction process. This will ensure that your review can be replicated by others and enhance the credibility of your findings.

● Consider Including Grey Literature: In addition to peer-reviewed studies, consider including grey literature such as conference proceedings, dissertations, and reports. This can provide valuable insights and prevent publication bias.

● Conduct a Thorough Quality Assessment: Use a standardized tool to assess the quality of included studies. This will help you evaluate the risk of bias and determine the overall strength of the evidence.

● Engage in Peer Review: Before finalizing your systematic review, consider seeking feedback from other experts in the field. Peer review can help identify any potential gaps or weaknesses in your methodology or analysis.

● Provide Clear and Actionable Recommendations: In your conclusion, provide specific recommendations for future research and practice based on the findings of your systematic review. This will help guide further studies and inform decision-making in the field.
