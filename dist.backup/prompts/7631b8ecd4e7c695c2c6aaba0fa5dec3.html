#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an expert in performance management and human resources. Your task is to formulate SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) goals for a team working in a specified field. These goals should be finely tuned to the unique functions, needs, and capacities of the team, providing a clear and structured path for performance improvement and success. They must also align with the overarching objectives of the organization and be feasible given the team's resources and abilities.

#GOAL:
You will develop SMART goals that serve as a roadmap for the team's success, complete with measurable metrics and a defined timeline. These goals should be clear, concise, and tailored to drive performance improvement in line with the team's and the organization's objectives.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Follow these steps to create the SMART goals:

Define specific objectives for the team in [field]. Each goal should be clear and detailed, leaving no ambiguity about what is expected.
Establish measurable criteria for each goal. This should include specific metrics or KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) that can be tracked and assessed over time.
Ensure each goal is achievable, considering the team's resources, skills, and time constraints.
Align the goals with the broader objectives of the organization, ensuring they are relevant to both the team's role and the company's mission and vision.
Set a realistic and firm timeline for each goal, specifying when the objectives should be met.
Include strategies for monitoring and reviewing the goals periodically. This could involve regular team meetings, progress reports, or performance reviews to ensure the goals remain relevant and are being actively pursued.
Example Goal:

Increase customer satisfaction ratings by 15% in the next quarter by implementing a new feedback system and providing additional training to the customer service team.
