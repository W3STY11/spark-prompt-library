#CONTEXT:
Adopt the role of an expert UI/UX designer with a deep understanding of color theory, psychology, and trends in web and app design. Your task is to suggest a color scheme for a [type of website/app] that is tailored to [target audience]. This color scheme should reflect the brand's identity and appeal to the target audience's preferences and emotions. It will need to be accessible, considering color blindness and other visual impairments, while also being contemporary and in line with current design trends.

#GOAL:
You will create a color scheme that enhances user experience, encourages engagement, and aligns with the brand's identity. This color scheme will be based on color psychology, accessibility standards, and design trends to ensure it appeals to the target audience and meets the brand's needs.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
Follow the step-by-step approach below to create the color scheme:

1. Analyze the brand's identity and values. Determine how these can be translated into color. For example, a brand focused on sustainability might lean towards earth tones, whereas a tech company might prefer cooler, more futuristic colors.
2. Consider the target audience's preferences and psychological responses to colors. Younger demographics might respond well to bright, vibrant colors, while a more mature audience might prefer subdued, sophisticated palettes.
3. Assess current design trends in the industry of the [type of website/app]. Identify whether minimalist color schemes, bold and vibrant colors, or pastel tones are in vogue.
4. Ensure the color scheme is accessible to all users, including those with visual impairments. Utilize tools like the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) to test color contrast and readability.
5. Create a primary color palette consisting of 2-3 main colors that reflect the brand's identity and appeal to the target audience. Explain the choice of each color and its psychological impact.
6. Select complementary secondary colors for accents and highlights. These should enhance the primary palette and be used sparingly to draw attention to key elements.
7. Provide visual examples of how these colors could be applied within the UI design, such as buttons, navigation bars, and backgrounds. If possible, include mock-ups or a color mood board.
