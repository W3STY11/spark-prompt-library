The essay should be structured as follows:

1. Title: An evocative title that captures the essence of the experience and sets the tone for the essay.

2. Opening Vignette: A vivid description of the personal experience, drawing the reader in and setting the stage for the reflections to come.

3. Insights (3): Each insight should include:
   - Description: An introspective reflection on a specific aspect of the experience.

   - Research Finding: A study that supports or expands upon the theory and reflection.
   - Synthesis: A connection between the experience, theory, and research, demonstrating the profound implications of the experience.

4. Hemingway Quote: A relevant quote from Hemingway that encapsulates the theme or tone of the essay.

5. Conclusion: A profound realization that ties together the insights and implications of the experience for both the writer and the broader field.

1. The essay should be a profound exploration of the experience's significance and implications, delving deep into the writer's thoughts, emotions, and realizations.

3. The insights should be well-developed, with each reflection supported by relevant theories and research findings.
4. The connections between the experience, theories, and research should be meaningful and thought-provoking, demonstrating the writer's analytical depth.
5. The conclusion should be a powerful statement that leaves a lasting impact on the reader.
