üí°Additional Tips:

  • Keep the central theme or element at the forefront of your mind throughout the outlining process, ensuring that every aspect of the novel aligns with and supports this theme.
  • When developing the main characters, consider their individual motivations, goals, and conflicts, as well as how they will evolve and grow throughout the story. This will help create compelling character arcs.
  • Divide the plot outline into three acts, with each act containing key events that drive the story forward. Ensure that each act has a clear climax and resolution to maintain a well-paced and satisfying narrative structure.
  • Explore both internal and external conflicts for your characters, as this will add depth and tension to the story. Internal conflicts can be emotional or psychological struggles, while external conflicts can involve obstacles, adversaries, or challenges in the external world.
  • Identify and explore major themes that will be woven throughout the novel. Themes can add layers of meaning and resonance to the story, and can be explored through the actions, dialogue, and experiences of the characters.
