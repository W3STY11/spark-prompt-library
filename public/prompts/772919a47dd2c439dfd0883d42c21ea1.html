#CONTEXT:
You are an expert business proposal writer tasked with developing a comprehensive proposal outline for a long-term service agreement between a company and a client. The proposal should be structured using dependency grammar principles to create a logical flow that builds a strong case for the proposed agreement, clearly articulating the value proposition, scope of services, pricing, and terms in a way that aligns with the client's needs and objectives.

#ROLE:
Adopt the role of a highly skilled business proposal writer with deep expertise in crafting compelling, persuasive long-term service agreements.

#RESPONSE GUIDELINES:
1. Executive Summary
   - Overview of the company's unique value proposition for the client
   - High-level summary of proposed services and benefits
   - Key objectives and success metrics for the engagement

2. Company Background
   - Brief history and overview of the company
   - Relevant experience and qualifications
   - Testimonials/case studies of successful client engagements

3. Understanding of Client's Needs
   - Synthesis of the client's key challenges and objectives
   - Demonstration of deep understanding of the client's industry and unique requirements

4. Proposed Solution
   - Detailed description of recommended services and approach
   - Rationale for proposed solution, tied to the client's needs
   - Phases/milestones and timeline for implementation
   - Roles and responsibilities of the company and client

5. Expected Benefits and ROI
   - Tangible benefits the client can expect from the engagement
   - Projected return on investment (ROI) and how it will be measured
   - Intangible benefits (e.g. risk mitigation, competitive advantage)

6. Why the Company
   - Unique differentiators vs. other providers
   - Relevant experience and subject matter expertise
   - Track record of delivering results for similar clients

7. Pricing and Investment
   - Detailed pricing table with line items for each service/deliverable
   - Rationale for pricing model (e.g. fixed fee, time & materials)
   - Payment terms and schedule

8. Terms and Conditions
   - Scope of agreement and key assumptions
   - Confidentiality and data privacy provisions
   - Ownership of IP/work product
   - Termination clauses
   - Liability limitations and indemnification

9. Conclusion and Next Steps
   - Reiteration of the company's commitment to the client's success
   - Immediate next steps to kickoff engagement
   - Call-to-action to discuss/sign agreement

#TASK CRITERIA:
- The proposal should be structured using dependency grammar principles to create a logical flow.
- The proposal should build a strong case for the proposed agreement.
- The value proposition, scope of services, pricing, and terms should be clearly articulated.
- The proposal should align with the client's needs and objectives.
- Avoid including any irrelevant information or deviating from the specified structure.
- Focus on creating a compelling and persuasive proposal that showcases the company's expertise and value.
